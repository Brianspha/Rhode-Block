Blocksmith Blockchain Hackathon

Blocksmith Blockchain Hackathon

Blocksmith Blockchain Hackathon is a week long Hackathon organised by Rhode-Block Blockchain Club Sudo Soc and Rhodes University The aim of the Hackathon is to encourage participants to think of the different ways Blockchain technology could be used to solve some of the problems faced by everyday society. During the Hackathon participants will be tasked to build eitheir build a working prototype which runs on the Ethereum Blockchain or Mockup a prototype and show how it would look if it were to be developed.
During the final day of the Hackathon there will be talks about Blockchain given by industry leaders

Rules

Hackathon Rules

  • The Hackathon will commence on the 15th of September 2018 17:00 pm, where participants will have to start building their prototypes .
  • Participants are not required to be present at the Hamilton Building they can build they prototypes from the comfort of their own homes.
  • Teams of 2-4 people must be formed inorder to participate in the Hackathon.
  • Teams will be required to check into the Hamilton Building on the 22nd of September at 12:00 pm Teams must have completed their prototypes by this time
  • On the 22nd of September 2018 teams will be given 10 minutes to pitch their idea to a panel of judges.
  • The breakdown of the final judgement is as follows 70% idea 20% prototype 10% collaboration and presentation
  • Winners will be announced after all the pitches have been completed

Judging Criteria

Idea

This is where you tell us the how, How is your solution going to solve the specific problem you've identified For instance Voting on the Blockchain.

How does using the Blockchain to vote help society or what are the advantages of using the Blockchain to vote

  • No actual person is involved in counting the votes its all automated
  • Votes cannot be tampared this goes back to one of the advantages of Blockchain which is its immutability So if someone were to modify something they would be essentially creating a new record of that thing
  • Transparency voting on the Blockhain introduces Transparency in the sense that the public can actually see the current state of the Vote totals as they are without errors
  • Elimintates duplicated votes or invalid votes
  • Voters can always change they votes
  • Voters have control of their votes
  • Votes can be verified easily trhough the use of transaction hashes

This is the Part where you would actually show us the different screens your prototype would have or rather taking us through a walkthorugh of how would utilize your prototype
